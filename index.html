2. MSc in Engineering in Biotechnology (DTU)

Because of my interest in bioprocess development, I decided to enroll in an MSc programme focused on fermentations. In this regard, there is no better place than the Technical University of Denmark (DTU), which is recognized worldwide for its excellence and long-standing leadership in the development of fermentation processes. For context, DTU is ranked as the 5th best university in the world in the field of biotechnology and the top technical university in Europe. What have I learned at DTU?

  • Bioprocess optimization and modelling strategies
  • Scale up/down procedures
  • GMP procedures
  • Practical experience in both laboratory and pilot scale

    • Here you will find the courses that I have taken during my MSc to have a better understanding of my learnings

3. Research Assistant at Agrobiomics

During my two years in the MSc programme, I combined my studies with a part-time job at Agrobiomics, where I initially worked in the Downstream & Analytical Department and later also became involved in the Upstream Department. My tasks at the company included:

  • Bioreactor set up and media preparation
  • Downstream experiments for metabolite recovery from fermentation broth
  • Routinary HPLC analysis and method development
  • Presentation of both bioreactor batch and downstream experiment results to managers

    • During my time at Agrobiomics, I’ve learned to be more flexible and to work effectively in multicultural environments, while always striving to solve as many problems as possible. You know, standard start-up life :)

4. MSc Thesis project at Bioneer

Because of my interest in expanding my bioprocess development expertise into mammalian cell culture, I had the opportunity to join Bioneer, a CDMO company, where I am working on a project focused on optimizing fed-batch intensified processes for CHO cell culture. As part of this project, I will carry out the following activities:

  • Application of DoE to optimize process intensification
  • Cell cultures in AMBR250 for optimization validation
  • Potential scale up to 2L vessel
